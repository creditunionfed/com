About Our Credit Union

Founded in 1965, Community Credit Union has been serving members with integrity and exceptional service. We're a not-for-profit financial cooperative owned by our members.

Our mission is to provide affordable financial services while helping members achieve their financial goals.

  • Member-owned, not for profit
  • Lower loan rates & higher savings yields
  • Local decision making
  • Personalized service

Frequently Asked Questions

A credit union is a not-for-profit financial cooperative owned by its members. Unlike banks, credit unions return profits to members in the form of lower fees, better rates, and improved services.

Membership is open to anyone who lives, works, worships, or attends school in our service area, as well as their immediate family members. Some employer groups and associations also qualify.

You can open an account online in minutes or visit any of our branches. You'll need a valid government-issued ID, Social Security number, and minimum opening deposit.

Yes, all deposits are insured up to $250,000 by the National Credit Union Administration (NCUA), a U.S. government agency.

You can access your accounts 24/7 through online banking, our mobile app, ATMs, phone banking, or by visiting one of our branches.
